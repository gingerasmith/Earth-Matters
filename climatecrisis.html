Here Are The Facts You Need To Know
Glaciers

The earth's average temperature has risen 1.9 degrees since the late 19th century. Our oceans have absorbed .4 degrees fahrenheit since 1969. Antartica has lost 127 billion tons of ice per year since 1993-2016 and that number has tripled since then. Greenland has lost 286 billion tons of ice between the years 1993-2016.


DEFORESTATION
 11% of emissions are due to deforestation, an average of 3.5-7 billions trees per year, that's the same as the population of the entire earth.

HUMAN EFFECTS

The ocean acidity has decreased in .1 pH since the industrial revolution, making it more acidic. In humans, a decrease in pH of .2-.3 could cause seizures, comas and even death. Imagine the effects that could do to the entire ocean.
NATURAL DISASTERS

*800 million people are affected by climate change effects such as flooding, heat waves, droughts and much more, that is more than the entire population of europe.

OUR OCEANS

Since the beginning of the industrial revolution, the acidity of the ocean water has increased by 30%. The sea level is rising 3.3 millimeters per year, that is 1.3 inches every decade. The global sea level has risen 8 inches in the last century.

HOW WE USE OUR ENERGY

85.2% of our energy is produced from oil, natural gas and coal.

EARTHS RESOURCES

Every year we blow this ecological budget earlier and earlier and at our current rate of resource use we'd need 1.75 planets to support our demand on Earth's ecosystem.Before the 1970s, our resource use remained within the boundaries of what our planet could produce - in 1961, we only used three-quarters of our annual resources.

INCREASING WARMTH

The past five years have been the five warmest since record-keeping began in the late 1800s. The Earth has experienced 42 straight years (since 1977) with an above-average global temperature

CARBON EMISSIONS

There is more carbon dioxide in the atmosphere today than at any point in the last 800,000 years.Though Americans make up just 4 percent of the world's population, we produce 25 percent of the carbon dioxide pollution from fossil-fuel burning -- by far the largest share of any country.*There are approximately 46 trillion pounds of fossil fuels produced per year.
